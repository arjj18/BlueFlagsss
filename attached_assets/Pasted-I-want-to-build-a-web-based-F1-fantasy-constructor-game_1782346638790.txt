I want to build a web-based F1 fantasy constructor game. Here is exactly how everything works:
The concept

Players build a dream F1 constructor by drafting 4 things in order — Driver 1, Driver 2, Engine, Car/Chassis. Each category has its own draft round. The game then simulates a 24 race season and tells you how many races your constructor won. The goal is to go a perfect 24-0.
The draft mechanic

Each draft round shows the player 5 randomly selected cards from that category. The 5 cards should always include a spread of budget ranges — roughly 1 cheap option (60-74 pts), 2 mid range options (75-87 pts), and 2 premium options (88-97 pts). The player picks one card. The other 4 disappear forever. Then the next category draft begins.
The player gets exactly ONE reroll per game — a button that dismisses all 5 current cards and generates 5 fresh ones. Once used it is gone for the rest of the game.
The budget system

Every item has a fixed rating out of 100 which is also its cost in budget points. The total budget cap is 240 points across all 4 picks combined. A budget bar at the top of the screen updates in real time after every pick showing how many points have been spent and how many remain. If the remaining budget makes it impossible to complete the team the game should warn the player before they confirm a pick.

The selection UI

Show 4 tabs across the top — Driver 1, Driver 2, Engine, Car. The active tab is highlighted. Each tab shows 5 draft cards in a grid. Each card shows the item name, era or nationality, and rating clearly. A budget bar sits at the top showing points spent vs 240 total. A reroll button is clearly visible and shows REROLL (1 remaining) — once used it says REROLL (0 remaining) and is greyed out. When a player picks a card it animates selected, then automatically advances to the next tab after a short delay.
After all 4 picks are made show a review screen displaying all 4 chosen items side by side with their ratings and total budget used. Show a CHANGE button on each pick so players can go back and redo that category draft with a new set of 5 cards — but this costs 10 budget points as a penalty each time. Show a big BUILD MY CONSTRUCTOR button to proceed to the simulation.
The simulation

Simulate 24 races one by one. Each race calculates a result based on the average rating of the 4 picks plus a random variance of plus or minus 15 points. If the adjusted score is 90 or above the constructor wins the race. If it is 80-89 they finish on the podium. If it is 65-79 they score points. Below 65 is a DNF or points-less finish.
Show each race result appearing one at a time with a short dramatic pause between each — like a ticker. Show the circuit name, the result, and a running win total.
The final results card

After all 24 races show a clean shareable result card showing:

The constructor name (generated from the picks e.g. Hamilton and Senna — Honda — McLaren MP4/4)
Each of the 4 picks and their ratings
Total budget used out of 240
Wins, podiums, points finishes and DNFs broken down
A headline result e.g. 18 wins from 24 races
A rating like Dominant, Competitive, Midfield, or Backmarker based on win total
A PLAY AGAIN button
